1. Confidential Information

“Confidential Information” means any and all information concerning either Party which has been or is, in the future, furnished by such Party (the “Provider”) or any of its representatives to the other Party (the “Recipient”) or any of its representatives, orally, electronically, in writing or otherwise, including, without limitation, information concerning its subsidiaries, businesses, operations, markets, products, product specifications, designs, documentation, technical data, trade secrets, processes, computer programs (in object or source code form), know-how, research and development, financial condition, strategies, marketing information, contracts, customers, white label partners, introducing brokers, employees and prospects. The term “Confidential Information” does not include information which the Recipient demonstrates: (i) was or becomes generally available to or known by the public without any violation of this Agreement on the part of the Recipient or any of its representatives; or (ii) was or becomes available to the Recipient or any of its representatives on a non-confidential basis prior to its disclosure to the Recipient by the Provider or its representatives; provided that the source of such information is not otherwise bound by a confidentiality agreement with the Provider or any of its representatives, or under a contractual, legal, fiduciary or other obligation to the Provider or any of its representatives not to transmit the information to the Recipient.

2. Limited & Use Closure

A Party shall use the Confidential Information of the other Party only for the purpose of evaluating whether to enter into a contractual business relationship with the other Party. A Party may disclose such Confidential Information only to its employees who (a) need to know such information and (b) are directly involved with such evaluation. A Party receiving Confidential Information in the form of computer software shall not copy, modify, translate, decompile, reverse engineer, disassemble, or perform a similar act on such computer software.
